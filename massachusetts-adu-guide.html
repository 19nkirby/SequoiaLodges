Can you actually build an ADU in Massachusetts now? Yes — here's how.

As of February 2025, Massachusetts changed the rules. Under the Affordable Homes Act, accessory dwelling units under 900 square feet are allowed by right in single-family zoning districts across the state — meaning most homeowners no longer need a special permit or a trip to the zoning board.

What “by-right” means for you

No variance or special permit required for a qualifying ADU (≤900 sq ft or 50% of your home's size, whichever is smaller)

Local zoning rules that conflict with the state ADU law are no longer enforceable

You still need a standard building permit, septic/sewer sign-off, and a Certificate of Occupancy
